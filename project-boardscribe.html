Boardscribe

A note transcription service for enhanced learning opportunities.

Background


A widespread problem for university students in our class is that our professors often write too quickly on the blackboard while talking and it is impossible to copy everything down while still getting a good understanding of the material being taught. There are often also cases where students are unable to attend class because they might be sick or have an interview. To solve this issue, we built a platform that takes care of the note taking for you - all you need is a picture of the board uploaded to the Boardscribe hub, to have neat notes transcribed for you in a Google Docs like format. Not only does this provide a single location for notes across multiple lectures, but it also provides opportunities for students and professors to interact, ask more questions offline, and get access to additional resources like videos.



How We Did It


The image recognition and machine learning were handled by Tessaract.js and Clarifai respectively. We were able to get pictures of individual characters out of pictures of text and pass those into Clarifai to transcribe into characters. We trained the AI extensively with over 1000 handwritten samples of different characters in order to be able to transcribe different handwriting styles. For the platform, we used the Django web framework and the SQLite database to store users notes' and organize them by class. Students also had the ability to comment on each note with suggestions and corrections by integrating their Facebook accounts.


The project can be found on both Github and on Devpost. Note that Boardscribe was built at a Hackathon, and like many hackathon projects, it comes with a range of bugs and issues that we wish we could have fixed or improved upon. There are also a ton of unanswered questions related to the accuracy of the OCR and potential next steps around standardizing the note formatting process.
